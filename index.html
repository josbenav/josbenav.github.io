About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Linguistics with a minor in Computational Linguistics (CL) at Indiana University specializing in syntax and semantics, with focused training in pragmatics, corpus annotation, and computational methods. My work investigates how speakers convey meaning beyond literal content (implicature, politeness, information structure) and how those phenomena can be reliably measured and annotated for computational evaluation.

I am an active member of the NLP Lab (Prof. Damir Cavar) and the Syntax–Semantics Reading Group (Profs. Emily Hanink & Tom Grano) at Indiana University.

Selected projects

Brief summaries

Annotation protocols for sociopragmatics

Designed codebooks and ran pilot annotations for conversational implicature and politeness markers; computed inter-annotator agreement and produced reproducible export scripts for analysis.

Question formation & clitic studies

Fieldwork and elicitation on Lutuv and Nariñense Andean Spanish to investigate question formation, clitic placement, and information-structuring diagnostics.
